# 💡 Strategy and Tips

> 📌 *Game as of **29 June 2026** (beta) — details may change.*

A practical playbook. None of this is mandatory — but it'll keep your dynasty alive and growing.

## The three golden rules

```mermaid
flowchart LR
    A[1 Keep the Powers<br/>off the extremes ⚖️] --> B[2 Always have heirs 👶]
    B --> C[3 Stay solvent 💰]
    C --> A
```

If you do nothing else, do these three. Almost every game-over comes from breaking one of them.

## Early game — survive (your first reigns)

- ⚖️ **Watch the four [[The Four Powers|Powers]].** Rescue any that nears empty; never let Church or Army max out.
- 💍 **Marry and have children fast.** A childless monarch is one bad event from extinction. See [[Marriage and Family]].
- 🧭 **Read the advisor hint**, then decide for your situation.
- 🏔️ **Consolidate in the north.** Don't dream of Granada in the year 760 — grow at home first.
- 🛌 **Rest** when stress is high and the realm is calm.

## Mid game — grow

- 🪜 **Climb the ladder** from count to duke to king, by [[War|war]], [[Diplomacy and Alliances|diplomacy]] or [[Intrigue and Schemes|intrigue]].
- 📜 **Keep a claim maturing** so you're always ready to pounce when an opportunity opens.
- 🤝 **Lock in an ally** (often by marriage) before a big war.
- 🪑 **Spread offices and marriages** among the powerful houses to keep them loyal ([[Noble Houses and Vassals]]).
- 💰 **Invest in your economy** during peace so you can afford war later.
- ⚔️ **Recruit only the [[Armies and Men-at-Arms|men-at-arms]] you can pay** every season.

## Late game — unify Hispania

- 🕰️ **Wait for al-Andalus to fragment**, then push south through the taifas.
- 🏰 **Aim for Granada before 1492** — plan the endgame several reigns ahead.
- 🏵️ **Invest in permanent tracks** ([[Dynasty Legacy|legacies]], [[Culture and Innovations|innovations]], [[Doctrines and Excommunication|doctrines]]) for a compounding edge.
- ⛪ **Convert and stabilise** newly conquered lands so they don't revolt.

## Common mistakes to avoid

- ❌ Letting the **Army** max out → civil war.
- ❌ Funding wars entirely on **debt** → bankruptcy.
- ❌ Relying on a **single sickly heir** → extinction to one plague.
- ❌ Ruling **arbitrarily** → tyranny, factions, excommunication.
- ❌ Only swiping cards and never **governing** → court neglect (see [[The Royal Court]]).

## Mindset

> [!tip] Play the dynasty, not the king
> Your current monarch *will* die. Judge your success by where the **family** stands in 1492, not by one heroic reign. Steady, moderate, well-married rule beats brilliant recklessness over the long run.
