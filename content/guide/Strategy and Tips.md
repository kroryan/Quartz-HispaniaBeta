# Strategy and Tips

> *Game as of **30 June 2026** (beta) - details may change.*

A practical playbook. None of this is mandatory, but it will keep your house alive and growing.

## The three golden rules

```mermaid
flowchart LR
    A[Keep the Powers<br/>off extremes] --> B[Always have heirs]
    B --> C[Stay solvent]
    C --> D[Stay landed]
    D --> A
```

Almost every game-over comes from breaking more than one of these at the same time.

## Pick the right start

- **Kingdom** is the best learning start: stronger resources, troops and no immediate need to climb.
- **Duchy** teaches vassal politics and title growth.
- **County** teaches claims, careful war and surviving under a liege.
- **Barony** is a recovery-and-ascent challenge from the first turn.
- **Islamic starts** change faith labels, marriage rules and the al-Andalus/taifa timeline.

See [[Choosing Your Start]].

## Early game - survive

- Watch the four [[The Four Powers|Powers]]. Rescue anything near empty and cool down religious authority or Army if they are too high.
- Secure marriage and children unless your chosen house already has a broad succession pool.
- Do one real governing action every few turns: appoint, build, pursue a claim, arrange a marriage, negotiate or manage a house.
- If starting below king, focus on the next title tier before dreaming of the whole peninsula.
- Avoid debt-funded wars before your economy can carry them.

## Mid game - grow

- Keep a claim maturing. A ready claim is an opportunity; an unprepared border is just a border.
- Use [[Diplomacy and Alliances|alliances]] before big wars, especially against stronger ranks.
- Build in your own counties. Development, forts and income make wars easier later.
- Use your [[Your Council|council]] deliberately: claims, relations, training, control, development, taxes, secrets and conversion all matter.
- Recruit only the [[Armies and Men-at-Arms|men-at-arms]] you can pay every season.

## Late game - unify Hispania

- After 1031, taifas open the south to diplomacy and conquest, but later outside powers can reabsorb weak Muslim-held taifas.
- Do not assume Granada alone is enough. Final sandbox victory is **every county under your house**.
- Invest in permanent tracks: [[Dynasty Legacy|legacies]], [[Culture and Innovations|innovations]] and [[Doctrines and Excommunication|doctrines]].
- Convert and stabilise conquered lands so they become productive, not a permanent unrest drain.

## Common mistakes

- Letting Army max out and then being surprised by civil war.
- Treating loans as income.
- Relying on one sickly heir.
- Picking a lower-rank start and trying to fight like a king.
- Ignoring faith differences after choosing an Islamic or Jewish line.
- Only swiping cards and never governing.

> [!tip] Play the house, not one ruler
> Your current ruler will die. Judge success by what the house owns, who can inherit, and whether the realm can pay for its ambitions.
