# ⚖️ The Four Powers

> 📌 *Game as of **29 June 2026** (beta) — details may change.*

Your survival rests on four **Powers**, shown as bars while you play. Almost every card moves at least one of them. Keep all four in a healthy middle range and you rule comfortably. Push any to an **extreme** and trouble follows.

![[main-screen.png]]
*The four Power bars sit at the top of the main screen, above the current event card.*

| Power | What it represents | Pleased by | Angered by |
|---|---|---|---|
| ⛪ **Church** | Clergy, faith, your holy legitimacy | Piety, building churches, supporting bishops | Impiety, taxing the Church, heresy |
| 🌾 **People** | The commoners, towns, public order | Justice, food, low taxes, mercy | Famine, cruelty, heavy taxes, war |
| ⚔️ **Army** | The nobles and your fighting strength | Glory, war, rewards for soldiers | Neglect, cowardice, disbanding troops |
| 💰 **Treasury** | The crown's wealth and finances | Trade, taxes, careful spending | Overspending, debt, costly wars |

## The golden rule: middle is safe

```mermaid
flowchart LR
    Z[Empty 0] -->|collapse risk| L[Low]
    L --> M[Healthy middle ✅]
    M --> H[High]
    H -->|domination risk| F[Full]
    style M fill:#1f3d2b,stroke:#5e6,color:#fff
    style Z fill:#5a1a1a,stroke:#e66,color:#fff
    style F fill:#5a4a1a,stroke:#ec6,color:#fff
```

- **A Power hitting empty (0)** is a crisis. The game gives you a chance or two to recover, but repeated unresolved crises **end your reign**. See [[Crises and Disasters]].
- **The Church or the Army growing *too* powerful** is also dangerous. An over-mighty Church can hold your crown hostage; an over-mighty Army can rise up in a **civil war** ([[War]]). Power you can't control will turn on you.

> [!tip] Don't chase perfect bars
> You can't keep all four maxed — cards constantly pull them in different directions, and that's the point. Aim to keep each one **off the extremes**, and rescue whichever is closest to danger.

## How the Powers connect to everything else

The four Powers are the visible "health bars," but they're fed by deeper systems:
- 💰 **Treasury** is tied to your real **gold and debt** in the [[Economy and Gold]] menu.
- ⚔️ **Army** reflects your nobles and your actual [[Armies and Men-at-Arms|troops]].
- ⛪ **Church** is shaped by your [[Faith and Religion|faith]] and your standing with the [[The Papacy|Pope]].
- 🌾 **People** rises and falls with justice, prosperity and the cost of your wars.

## A hidden fifth meter: conquest

Behind the scenes there's also a hidden **conquest** measure that tracks how much of Hispania you've united. You won't see it as a bar, but it's what drives the ultimate [[Winning and Losing|victory]]. Every province you take pushes it up.

## Your character matters

Who you are colours the realm. A ruler's **traits** subtly tug the Powers each season — a pious king lifts the Church, a warlike one strengthens the Army but unsettles the clergy, a greedy one drains the People. See [[Traits and Your Character]].

## Survival summary

1. 🔴 **Never let a Power sit at empty** — rescue it.
2. 🟡 **Never let Church or Army sit at full** — it will dominate you.
3. 🟢 **Keep everything in the middle** and you can focus on growing your dynasty and realm.
