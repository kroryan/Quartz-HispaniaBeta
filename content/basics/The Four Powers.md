# The Four Powers

> *Game as of **30 June 2026** (beta) - details may change.*

Your survival rests on four **Powers**, shown as bars while you play. Almost every card moves at least one of them. Keep them in a healthy middle range and you rule comfortably. Push any to an **extreme** and trouble follows.

![[main-screen.png]]
*The Power bars sit at the top of the main screen, above the current event card.*

| Power | What it represents | Pleased by | Angered by |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Church / Umma / Aljama** | Religious authority and legitimacy, named by your ruler's faith | Piety, endowments, conversions, religious learning | Impiety, abuse of holy institutions, neglect |
| **People** | Commoners, towns, public order and prosperity | Justice, food, low taxes, mercy | Famine, cruelty, heavy taxes, long wars |
| **Army** | Military backing, noble martial support and command strength | Glory, drilling, troops, rewards for soldiers | Neglect, cowardice, unpaid forces |
| **Treasury** | Financial health, gold flow and debt pressure | Trade, taxes, buildings, careful spending | Overspending, loans, war upkeep, insolvency |

## The golden rule: middle is safe

```mermaid
flowchart LR
    Z[Empty 0] -->|collapse risk| L[Low]
    L --> M[Healthy middle]
    M --> H[High]
    H -->|domination risk| F[Full]
```

- **A Power hitting empty** is a crisis. The game gives you a chance to recover, but repeated unresolved crises end the reign.
- **The religious Power or the Army growing too powerful** is also dangerous. An over-mighty religious institution can force concessions; an over-mighty Army can rise in civil war.

> [!tip] Do not chase perfect bars
> You cannot keep all four maxed. The game is about avoiding collapse and domination, not turning every meter green forever.

## How the Powers connect to deeper systems

- **Treasury** is tied to real gold, debt, income and upkeep in [[Economy and Gold]].
- **Army** is shaped by levies, men-at-arms, morale, war funds and [[Your Council|marshal]] work.
- **Church / Umma / Aljama** follows your faith, doctrines, conversion and papal status if Christian.
- **People** reflects prosperity, justice, unrest and the cost of war.

## Territory is separate

There is also a conquest layer behind the scenes: your map holdings and the Territory value. The final sandbox victory is not a bar hitting a number; it is your house **personally controlling every county on the peninsula**. See [[Winning and Losing]].

## Your character matters

Ruler traits subtly tug the realm over time. A pious ruler strengthens religious authority, a warlike one helps the Army but can unsettle others, and a greedy or cruel one strains the People. See [[Traits and Your Character]].

## Survival summary

1. **Never leave a Power at empty** - rescue it.
2. **Never let religion or Army sit at full** - dominance will turn against you.
3. **Keep the middle stable** so you can focus on dynasty, economy and conquest.
